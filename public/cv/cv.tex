\cvsection{\href{https://github.com/luisacicolini}{Projects}}

\cvevent{Formalized Semantics For Hardware Verification}{}{September 2024 - present}{}
My research targets the \textbf{formalization of hardware abstractions in Lean}.
I am interested in \textbf{bridging maths and hardware verification}, giving formalized (and formally verifiable!)
semantics to hardware concepts and building the infrastructure necessary to enable reasoning about 
these concepts from a hardware perspective. 
The two abstractions I work with at the moment are \textbf{bitvectors}, for the formalization of combinational logic, and \textbf{coinduction}, 
the latency-insensitive abstractions. \\
\smallskip
\cvevent{Formalized RISC-V Instruction Set Architecture}{}{March 2025 - present}{}
I contribute to the development of a \href{https://github.com/opencompl/riscv-lean}{\textbf{fully-bitblastable library for the RISC-V Instruction Set}}, proven equivalent to the Sail processor model.\\
\smallskip
\cvevent{\texttt{BitVec} in Lean4}{}{July 2024 - present}{}
I am among \textbf{\href{https://github.com/leanprover/lean4}{Lean4}'s top 30 contributors}, 
working on the development of Lean's bitvector library and verified bitblaster.\\
\smallskip
\cvevent{Model Checking For CIRCT}{}{September 2023 - present}{}
I worked on formalizing the semantics of \textbf{\href{https://circt.llvm.org}{CIRCT}'s Finite State Machine (FSM) dialect}, 
enabling CIRCT to integrate verification at a higher level of the stack. \\
\smallskip
\cvevent{Accelerating Regular Expressions Matching}{}{March 2022 - September 2023}{}
I worked on the \textbf{Multi-Finite-State-Automaton} model, aimed at accelerating regex pattern matching.
